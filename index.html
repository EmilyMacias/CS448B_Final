Demistifying Investment


Investing is a key part of financial success, being the differentiating factor from holding onto our money and having it lose value versus having it grow as inflation increases over the years. So why don't more people do it? Who is empowered to invest early on in their lives and who is not? What are these contributing factors?

Women invest less than men. According to a 2021 study by NerdWallet, compared to 66% of men who invest in the stock market, only 48% of women invest. Investing is commonly regarded as "risky" or "dangerous" which if done incorrectly, can often be true. Many sites walk you through the basics of investing, but the goal for us is to demystify it and clear up common misconceptions about how investments can look through visual exploration.

Let's learn what it looks like to invest properly, why it can be so powerful, and what the current investing landscape looks like. To do this we will explore 3 main topics:

  • What generational wealth is and how investments contribute
  • What investing can actually look like safely
  • What the investing landscape looks like in terms of who invests and how much

To begin, let's talk about what an investment even is. Investments can take many different forms, from traditional stock holdings to real estate investments. Both of these contribute to what we call generational wealth, where money is passed down between generations of a family, primarily in these investment forms.

Alternatively, a common way to gain interest on your money is via a HYSA (High Yield Savings Accounts) which is not an investment but rather a savings account with a high interest rate. Unlike investments in the stock market, you cannot lose money because the interest rate will never go below zero, however, the trade-off is that this additional safety comes with a much lower reward than is possible with stock investments. Let's take a look at this ourselves and explore our possibilities for losses and rewards.

Real Estate Investments

Now that we have gotten the chance to explore investment through avenues like HYSA accounts and the S&P500, let's understand where else the idea of built, generational wealth comes from: real estate. This does not mean that you must buy and flip homes, although it can, rather that home ownership throughout generations is a key source of wealth for families and can lead to growth.
